RESEARCH: REAL-WORLD PROBLEMS + WINNING ZK HACKATHON PROJECTS (for Stellar Hacks: Real-World ZK)
Compiled 2026-07-02. Deadline: 2026/07/03 22:30.

Meta-finding: Stellar's Protocol 25 "X-Ray" and Protocol 26 "Yardstick" added native BN254 curve ops
and Poseidon/Poseidon2 hashing host functions specifically to make on-chain ZK verification cheap.
Judges will likely reward projects that specifically exploit these native host functions (fast Poseidon
Merkle trees, cheap BN254/Groth16 pairing checks) over generic ZK demos that ignore them.
Caveat: at time of the earlier "Stellar Hacks: ZK Gaming" event, Noir support on Soroban was described
as still limited by decentralized-computing constraints — verify current Noir/UltraHonk verifier maturity
before committing to a Noir-heavy "wild" scope; keep Circom or RISC Zero as fallback.

================================================================================
PART A — REAL-WORLD PROBLEMS ZK CAN SOLVE (sourced framing, not hackathon projects)
================================================================================

1. CROSS-BORDER REMITTANCE COMPLIANCE
   Problem: compliance ops are ~10-15% of international banks' operational cost; correspondent banking
   is slow, costly, opaque. Proposed fix in literature: blockchain + self-sovereign identity + ZKPs so
   smart contracts embed zk-KYC, sanctions screening, and reporting directly into payment rails without
   exposing customer data.
   Sources: jisem-journal.com / lorojournals.com "Zero-Knowledge Enabled Cross-Border Payment Systems";
   tookitaki.com on remittance/correspondent-banking money-laundering risk.
   Stellar fit (WILD): Noir circuit proving sender/receiver pass jurisdiction + sanctions-list checks
   (nullifier-based), verified via a Soroban Groth16/UltraHonk verifier before releasing a Stellar
   payment; pair with an off-chain ASP-style allowlist.

2. PAYROLL / INVOICING PRIVACY
   Problem: companies must prove tax/labor-law compliance without exposing individual salaries.
   Source: OneSafe, "Current Trends in Crypto Payroll Solutions."
   Stellar fit (MILD-MEDIUM): Circom/Noir circuit proving "sum of payouts == treasury withdrawal" AND
   "each payout >= minimum wage" via range proof, hiding individual amounts; Soroban verifier gates the
   batch payment contract.

3. SUPPLY CHAIN PROVENANCE WITH CONFIDENTIALITY
   Problem: need to validate authenticity/certifications without revealing proprietary supplier data.
   Real implementation: Everledger (diamonds/wine/art provenance).
   Sources: cryptoslate.com "Beyond Privacy: Zero-knowledge applications in supply chain transparency";
   IBM Research, "Enabling Privacy and Traceability in Supply Chains using Blockchain and ZKPs."
   Stellar fit (WILD): multi-hop Merkle-based provenance chain, each handoff proven in Noir, final proof
   aggregated/verified in Soroban to gate RWA-token issuance.

4. GIG WORKER CREDENTIAL / REPUTATION VERIFICATION
   Problem: platforms prioritize onboarding speed over rigor (exploitable identity gaps); GDPR/CCPA
   raise the bar for storing credential data. ZKPs let workers prove license/background-check status
   without revealing underlying documents.
   Sources: AuthBridge, Didit, gcheck (industry trend pieces); academic paper "A ZK-Proof-Enabled
   Blockchain-Based Academic Record Verification System" (PMC).
   Stellar fit (MILD): Noir circuit proving "credential issued by trusted issuer + not expired" via a
   Merkle tree of issuer signatures, verified on Soroban before granting a gig-marketplace access badge
   (nullifier prevents reuse/Sybil).

5. CONFIDENTIAL LENDING / DEFI PRIVACY
   Problem (Cointelegraph: "Confidential lending will unlock trillions for DeFi markets"): transparent
   on-chain positions let others front-run/copy strategies and expose institutions' risk books.
   Stellar fit (WILD): private collateralization-ratio proof (range proof) gating a Soroban lending pool
   draw; oracle price feed committed via hash; RISC Zero proves off-chain solvency computation.

PART B — REAL HACKATHON-WINNING / NOTABLE PROJECTS (mined for adaptation to Stellar)

--- IDENTITY / AGE / KYC ---

1. Identifi — ETHGlobal Taipei, won World Pool Prize + Celo L2 Pool Prize.
   What: Self Protocol ZK attestations (age/nationality/socials) + Worldcoin biometrics; server verifies
   proofs without seeing raw personal data.
   Why it won: solves "prove personhood/age without doxxing" cleanly using existing SDKs.
   Stellar port (MILD): Noir circuit verifying a Self-Protocol-style passport/NFC attestation signature,
   outputs boolean "over 18" claim consumed by a Soroban gate.
   Source: https://ethglobal.com/showcase/identifi-hbpyp

2. Brick Towers — Midnight "ZK Identity" hackathon winner.
   What: ZK proof of legal drinking age for alcohol e-commerce, no birthdate/ID exposure.
   Why it won: concrete regulated-commerce use case.
   Stellar port (MILD, near 1:1 with Stellar's own "age/eligibility" idea): Circom circuit comparing
   birthdate-hash range vs. current date; Soroban verifier unlocks a purchase contract.
   Source: https://midnight.network/blog/zk-identity-hackathon-teams-showcase-decentralized-identity

3. Edda Labs — same Midnight hackathon. Compliance-gated real estate auction; modular contracts prove
   AML/jurisdiction compliance before allowing auction participation.
   Stellar port (WILD): combine sealed-bid auction (#19) + jurisdiction Merkle-allowlist proof.

4. Fairway — same hackathon. Government-ID-bound soulbound token via Midnight.
   Stellar port (MEDIUM): Noir circuit verifies a signed government-ID hash, issues a nullifier-bound
   non-transferable Soroban token — prevents duplicate identities. Direct analog to Stellar's own
   "privacy-preserving on-chain identity / Sybil resistance via nullifiers" idea.

5. Midnames — same hackathon, honorable mention. NFC passport chip scan -> ZK proof of passport
   validity/expiry; three-part architecture (local server, mobile app, dApp).
   Stellar port (WILD, multi-part): mobile NFC read + Noir proof generation + Soroban verifier —
   ambitious for a weekend, good "wild" pick.

6. zkCV — ETHGlobal Circuit Breaker (2024). Anonymous CV/job application eliminating hiring bias —
   proves qualifications without revealing name/age/gender.
   Stellar port (MILD): Circom Merkle-membership proof ("holds a degree/certification from an
   accredited issuer"), verified on Soroban, outputs only pass/fail.

7. ZKPassport — originated hackathon/PSE-adjacent, acquired by Aztec Labs (2025) to power privacy-first
   identity on Ethereum. Real government-ID-attested proofs from ePassport NFC chips via ZK. One of the
   most "real" (now production, VC/Aztec-backed) examples in this space.
   Stellar port (WILD full; MILD if reusing their SDK output): full passport-to-Soroban integration is
   ambitious — a mild version just builds the Soroban verifier + a consuming dApp using ZKPassport's
   existing proof output (e.g., "prove nationality != sanctioned list" for an RWA issuer).
   Sources: https://zkpassport.id/, https://crypto.news/aztec-labs-acquires-zkpassport-code-stays-open/

8. Anon Aadhaar — PSE/Ethereum Foundation project, widely reused as a hackathon building block (e.g.
   "Anon Checkin" at ETHIndia). ZK proof of Indian national-ID ownership revealing only chosen
   attributes. Real-world relevance: India's 1.4B-person Aadhaar system.
   Stellar port (MEDIUM): port the Circom circuit logic (RSA signature verification over Aadhaar QR
   data) to prove residency/age, verify via Soroban.
   Sources: https://github.com/anon-aadhaar/anon-aadhaar, https://pse.dev/projects/anon-aadhaar

9. BlockCircle — zk-based KYC/creditworthiness using Reclaim Protocol (zkTLS) over off-chain data (UPI,
   banking, salary slips) with zero data disclosure; targets underbanked Sybil-resistant onboarding.
   Stellar port (WILD): zkTLS proof of a bank API response (e.g. balance >= X) verified via a RISC Zero
   guest program, Groth16-wrapped and checked in Soroban — a strong "proof-of-funds" implementation.
   Source: https://www.hackquest.io/projects/BlockCircle

10. zkCreditScore (zkMe) — anonymous bridging of US FICO credit scores on-chain using zkTLS + Reclaim
    Protocol; a real product, not just a demo.
    Stellar port (MEDIUM): mild version proves "FICO score fetched via zkTLS >= threshold" using a fixed
    range-proof circuit, consumed by a Soroban lending contract for undercollateralized loans.
    Sources: https://medium.com/@zkMe/zkme-launches-zkcreditscore-anonymous-bridging-of-credit-scores-onchain-2b15778fc481,
    https://zkscore.credit/

11. DAO Opener — DIF Hackathon winner. Proves NFT-gated DAO membership without holding the NFT's
    private key on the verifying device, using Polygon ID.
    Stellar port (MILD, near 1:1 with Stellar's own "private allowlist" idea): Merkle-membership circuit
    proving "wallet holds one of N allowlisted NFTs/tokens" without revealing which one.

--- PRIVATE PAYMENTS / SHIELDED TRANSFERS / CONFIDENTIAL TOKENS ---

12. Roman Kyoto — ETHGlobal Sydney 2024 finalist, won dabl.club "Launch an MVP on Polygon Cardona"
    (1st place). Multi-asset shielded pool (MASP) for ERC-20s using Circom circuits + UTXO note model
    with encrypted notes in a Merkle tree (Tornado-Nova-like but multi-asset). Deployed to Base Sepolia +
    Polygon Cardona for cheap proving.
    Why compelling: solves "shielded pool for many tokens, not just one" — a real gap vs. Tornado Cash.
    Stellar port (WILD — strong flagship candidate): Circom UTXO note circuit (nullifier + commitment
    scheme), Merkle tree of commitments maintained by a Soroban contract, Groth16 verifier checks spend
    proofs. Maps directly onto Stellar's own "UTXO-style private payment systems" and "shielded
    stablecoin wallet" ideas.
    Source: https://ethglobal.com/showcase/roman-kyoto-s3uio

13. shadefi / Darkpool / Mystic DEX / CryptoHold'em — ETHGlobal London 2024, FHE-based (Fhenix) private
    DeFi/orderbook/poker projects. Not Groth16/Circom, but shows demand for "hidden balances, public
    addresses" DEX orderbooks.
    Stellar note: no FHE on Stellar — ZK equivalent is a private order-matching circuit (Noir) proving a
    batch auction clears without revealing individual order sizes ("confidential DEX with private
    positions").

14. Privacy GHO Token — LFGHO 2024 (Aave hackathon). Encrypted balance transfers for the GHO stablecoin.
    Direct precedent for "fully shielded stablecoin wallets."
    Stellar port (MEDIUM): shielded-balance wrapper around a Stellar stablecoin/SEP-41 token using a
    Roman-Kyoto-style UTXO circuit, single-asset for a mild weekend scope.

15. ZKP2P (zkp2p) — conceived at ZK Hack Lisbon 2023 (2nd place); now a live, funded protocol (PSE
    grants). Trustless P2P fiat on/offramp using zkTLS: user proves they made an off-chain Venmo/bank
    payment without revealing full transaction details; escrow releases crypto automatically.
    Why compelling: solves actual on/offramp friction — one of the strongest "real-world ZK" stories.
    Stellar port (WILD, flagship candidate): RISC Zero guest program verifies a TLS transcript of a
    payment-provider API response (Wise, PayPal), Groth16-wraps the receipt, Soroban escrow releases
    XLM/stablecoin. Combines zkTLS + RISC Zero + Soroban escrow.
    Sources: https://www.dlnews.com/research/internal/zkp2p-core-contributor-on-trustless-fiat-ramps-and-the-power-of-zk-tls/,
    https://github.com/zkp2p

16. Confido — EthCC Brussels 2024. Wage-payment confidentiality via functional encryption. Direct
    precedent for "confidential payroll."
    Stellar port (MILD): simpler ZK version — range-proof circuit proving each payout meets minimum-
    wage/contract terms without revealing exact amount, batch-verified in Soroban.

17. Cloak Coin, toadnado, Shadow Pay — various 2024 hackathon L1/L2 mixers and P2P confidential-transfer
    apps (per web3privacy list). Reinforce demand for simple shielded-transfer weekend projects.
    Stellar port (MILD — best "safe first ZK project" pick): single-asset Tornado-style Noir circuit
    (commitment/nullifier, fixed denominations) with a Soroban Merkle-tree + verifier contract.

--- COMPLIANCE-AWARE PRIVACY / PRIVACY POOLS ---

18. Privacy Pools (0xbow) — not a hackathon project; the production reference architecture for
    "compliant privacy pools with ASP allow/deny lists," live on Ethereum mainnet (Vitalik Buterin among
    first users). Directly matches Stellar's own idea-list item.
    How it works: users deposit into a shielded pool; withdrawal requires a ZK proof that the deposit is
    a member of an "association set" (ASP-curated allowlist) via Merkle inclusion, proving legitimacy
    without revealing which deposit is whose.
    Stellar port (WILD, probably the single best flagship pick — matches 2 of Stellar's own listed ideas
    and has a live reference to study): port the ASP Merkle-membership + nullifier circuit to
    Circom/Noir, verify through Soroban; ASP maintained off-chain by a compliance oracle updating an
    on-chain Merkle root.
    Sources: https://privacypools.com/, https://docs.privacypools.com/,
    https://github.com/0xbow-io/privacy-pools-core

--- SEALED-BID AUCTIONS / VOTING ---

19. Blind Spot Auction — ETHGlobal showcase project. First-price sealed-bid auction for NFT art; ZK
    hides bids until reveal, preventing price gouging/manipulation.
    Stellar port (MILD): Noir circuit commits to a bid hash at bid-time, later proves bid >= threshold or
    matches the commitment at reveal-time; Soroban contract manages commit/reveal phases + settlement.
    Source: https://ethglobal.com/showcase/blind-spot-auction-zeffz

20. FHE Vickrey Auction — Scaling Ethereum 2024 (FHE-based, not ZK-SNARK, but same auction-privacy
    pattern; Vickrey = second-price sealed bid). Useful design reference despite different primitive.

21. PolyVOTE — MACI-based private voting platform (MPC + ZK), deploys MACI on Linea, whitelabel UI for
    election creation. Direct precedent for "private DAO governance/voting."
    Stellar port (WILD if full MACI; MILD/MEDIUM simplified): full MACI port is nontrivial — a simpler
    version drops the anti-collusion coordinator and does Semaphore-style anonymous-vote-with-nullifier
    in Noir, verified on Soroban — still meets "private voting" without MACI's full complexity.

22. zkdao — Devpost project. Attach any DAO to a ZKDAO contract for fully anonymous proposal voting.
    Similar pattern to Semaphore.

23. NEVO — ETHDam 2024. Privacy-enhanced voting combining the MACI protocol. Reinforces MACI as the
    reference architecture judges reward for DAO governance privacy.

24. zkVoting / zkDemocracy / zkvote — multiple independent hackathon PoCs, Semaphore + Polygon ID combos
    for anonymous voting; simplest reference implementations for a mild-scope Stellar port.
    Sources: https://github.com/TheBojda/zkdemocracy, https://github.com/flyingnobita/zkvote

--- VERIFIABLE OFF-CHAIN COMPUTATION (credit scores, game results, ML) ---

25. z0Wallet — Scaling Ethereum 2024 (ETHGlobal). Privacy-focused multisig wallet powered by the RISC
    Zero (risc0) stack. Directly relevant as a RISC Zero + Soroban pattern reference.
    Stellar port (MEDIUM): RISC Zero guest program validates multisig approval logic off-chain,
    Groth16-wraps proof, Soroban verifier contract executes the approved transaction.

26. zkora — ETHGlobal, RISC Zero-based. Developers write Solidity + JS that runs inside a zkVM; the JS
    execution result + proof of correct execution get verified on-chain to validate off-chain
    computation. Essentially a template for "verifiable off-chain computation" — arguably the cleanest
    RISC Zero pattern to copy.
    Stellar port (MEDIUM-WILD): swap Solidity target for a Soroban contract call; RISC Zero zkVM executes
    an arbitrary off-chain scoring/ML-inference function, journal output + Groth16 proof verified by
    Soroban before the contract accepts the result.
    Source: https://web.ethglobal.com/showcase/zkora-r3dej

27. ZkProof Portal — RISC Zero zkVM proof verifying correctness of an off-chain order-matching process
    (DEX-adjacent verifiable computation).
    Source: https://ethglobal.com/showcase/zkproof-portal-1jjg5

28. Anti-Sandwich — RISC Zero project verifying canonical transaction ordering in a multicall execution,
    proving MEV-resistant ordering happened off-chain. Good "verifiable computation for fairness"
    pattern.
    Source: https://ethglobal.com/showcase/anti-sandwich-q9gmv

29. ZTF — used RISC Zero's STARK-based zkVM plus Bonsai to convert STARK->SNARK for cheap on-chain
    verification — exactly the RISC Zero -> Groth16 -> Soroban pattern this hackathon calls for. Directly
    relevant technical precedent.

30. zk-credit-score-eth-global-bangkok (GitHub repo, multiple similar hackathon PoCs) — proves a
    borrower meets a credit-score threshold without revealing the score, via ECC/range-proof circuits.
    Direct precedent for Stellar's "verifiable off-chain computation: credit scores" idea.
    Source: https://github.com/ritiklakhwani/zk-credit-score-eth-global-bangkok

--- DEFI PRIVACY / LENDING ---

31. Eclipse — winner of the $100K Aleo Hackathon (Paris). Private lending platform; lending circuits in
    Leo (Aleo's ZK-native language), custom oracle generates off-chain proofs that collateral is
    sufficient without revealing amounts.
    Why it won (likely judge reasoning): solved a real DeFi transparency problem (front-running/
    copy-trading of visible positions) with a working custom oracle, not just a toy circuit.
    Stellar port (WILD): Noir circuit proving collateralization ratio >= threshold via range proof on
    hidden collateral/debt values; oracle price commitment verified via hash; Soroban lending pool gates
    borrow/liquidation using the verifier.
    Source: https://medium.com/@alexandregrondin7/eclipse-redefining-defi-lending-with-privacy-on-aleo-ab77ada0997d

32. Ghost Finance — private lending/borrowing protocol matching lenders/borrowers privately, hiding
    negotiated rates. Reinforces "confidential DeFi" as a repeatable winning idea across hackathons.

--- PROOF-OF-RESERVES ---

37. Zero-Knowledge Proof Hackathon (BSV, Nov-Dec 2022) — top 3 winners (BLS12-381 Library, ZkBaguette, ZK
    Minesweeper) split $45K; broader theme areas included proof-of-reserves. No single named
    "proof-of-reserves" winner surfaced, but confirms it as an established, judge-rewarded ZK hackathon
    category. Academic precedent: Xiang Fu, "Instant Zero Knowledge Proof of Reserve" (2023).
    Stellar port (MILD-MEDIUM): Merkle-sum-tree circuit (Circom) where an issuer proves total
    liabilities <= on-chain reserve balance without revealing individual account balances — classic
    exchange/stablecoin proof-of-reserves pattern, well-suited to a Stellar RWA/stablecoin issuer.

--- CROSS-CHAIN / RECURSIVE PROOFS ---

38. zkBridge — academic/production reference (not hackathon-specific, but powers a dedicated "zkBridge
    track" at Berkeley RDI's zkp-web3-hackathon). Trustless cross-chain bridging using recursive SNARKs
    to prove source-chain state transitions to a destination chain.
    Why it's relevant to THIS hackathon specifically: directly matches Stellar's own "cross-chain
    private bridges using BN254 compatibility" idea, and Stellar's Protocol 25/26 upgrades added native
    BN254 curve ops + Poseidon hashing host functions specifically to make this cheap.
    Stellar port (WILD, flagship): prove an Ethereum light-client state transition or event-inclusion
    proof using a Circom/Noir circuit over BN254, verify on Soroban using the new native host functions —
    arguably the most "on-brand" wild project given Stellar's own protocol investment.
    Sources: https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.00264, https://docs.zkbridge.com/,
    https://rdi.berkeley.edu/zkp-web3-hackathon/tracks/zk_bridge_track/

39. SnarkPack / SnarkFold / HEKATON — research references (not hackathons). Practical Groth16
    proof-aggregation schemes (SnarkPack aggregates 8192 proofs in 8.7s, verifies in 163ms). Useful
    technical grounding for a "proof aggregation" wild project: batch many small Groth16 proofs (e.g.
    many users' age-verification proofs) into one aggregate proof, cutting Soroban verification gas
    costs.
    Sources: https://eprint.iacr.org/2021/529.pdf, https://eprint.iacr.org/2023/1946.pdf

================================================================================
SHORTLIST — MILD vs WILD PICKS FOR THIS HACKATHON
================================================================================

MILD (weekend, single circuit, low execution risk):
  - Age/eligibility verification (Brick Towers pattern) — Circom/Noir + Soroban boolean gate
  - Merkle-membership private allowlist (DAO Opener pattern)
  - Commit-reveal sealed-bid auction (Blind Spot Auction pattern)
  - Single-asset shielded transfer (Cloak Coin / Roman-Kyoto-lite pattern)
  - Proof-of-reserves via Merkle-sum-tree

WILD (ambitious, multi-part, high payoff, best differentiation):
  - Compliant privacy pool with ASP allow/deny list (0xbow Privacy Pools port) — matches 2 of Stellar's
    own listed ideas directly, has a live reference implementation to study
  - Multi-asset shielded pool / UTXO payment system (Roman Kyoto full port)
  - zkTLS-based P2P fiat on/offramp using RISC Zero (ZKP2P pattern)
  - Cross-chain BN254 light-client bridge leveraging Stellar's new native host functions (zkBridge
    pattern) — most "on-brand" given Stellar's own protocol investment in Protocol 25/26
  - Verifiable off-chain ML/credit-score computation via RISC Zero zkVM + Groth16 + Soroban (zkora
    pattern)

Note on sourcing: URLs above are as found by the research pass; verify directly before citing in a
submission, and independently confirm any item flagged "not fully confirmed" (e.g. priv.cast).
